About this resource

LangPedagogy is a digital knowledge hub dedicated to the art and science of teaching languages. Built for teacher trainers, graduate students, and practicing educators, the site bridges theory and practice in second/foreign language education.

Our mission: demystify key methodologies, promote inclusive classrooms, and foster reflective teaching. Each chapter includes core concepts, illustrative examples, and reflective prompts.

📌 Why pedagogy of language?

Language teaching has evolved from grammar-translation to communicative, task-based, and plurilingual approaches. Staying updated ensures that learners thrive in multilingual, intercultural environments.

Chapter 2

Second Language Acquisition Theories

Krashen's Monitor Model (acquisition vs. learning), Interaction Hypothesis (Long), Sociocultural Theory (Vygotsky, ZPD), and Skill Acquisition Theory (DeKeyser). Understanding how learners internalize grammar and lexis.

Classroom implications

  • Creating low-anxiety environments
  • Scaffolding and collaborative dialogue
  • Noticing and feedback mechanisms
Chapter 3

Communicative Language Teaching (CLT)

Fluency over accuracy, real-life tasks, information gap activities, and authentic materials. CLT principles: meaningful communication as both the means and goal of learning.

Activity examples

Role plays, simulations, debates, jigsaw listening, and problem-solving tasks. CLT 2.0 integrates digital storytelling.

Chapter 4

Task-Based Language Teaching (TBLT)

Designing pedagogic tasks: pre-task, task cycle, language focus. Real-world tasks like planning a trip, making recommendations, solving a puzzle. Evidence from Ellis & Skehan.

Benefits

Promotes negotiation of meaning, learner autonomy, and transferable skills. Ideal for ESP and integrated skills classrooms.

Chapter 5

Grammar Instruction Approaches

Inductive vs deductive teaching; focus on form vs focus on forms; processing instruction (VanPatten); consciousness-raising tasks. Balancing explicit explanation with communicative practice.

Practical strategies

  • Dictogloss
  • Textual enhancement
  • Interactive grammar games
Chapter 6

Vocabulary Acquisition Strategies

Lexical approach (Lewis), spacing effect, retrieval practice, word families, and collocations. Explicit instruction plus extensive reading/viewing. Tools: flashcards, corpora, and gamification.

📖 High-frequency word lists (e.g., New General Service List) and deliberate vocabulary instruction double retention rates.
Chapter 7

Technology in Language Learning

CALL, MALL, AI chatbots, gamified apps (Duolingo, Quizlet), virtual exchange, and corpora tools. Digital equity and teacher digital competence as key factors.

Emerging trends

Generative AI for personalized dialogues, automatic speech recognition, and VR immersion.

Chapter 8

Assessment and Feedback

Formative vs summative, diagnostic assessment, dynamic assessment, portfolio use. Feedback types: corrective, metalinguistic, recasts, and peer feedback. Aligning assessment with learning objectives.

Best practices

Rubrics, self-assessment checklists, and feed-forward strategies to empower learners.

Chapter 9

Multilingualism & Translanguaging

Embracing learners' full linguistic repertoire as a resource. Translanguaging pedagogy (García & Wei) promotes cross-linguistic connections, code-meshing, and identity affirmation.

Classroom strategies: bilingual glossaries, multilingual word walls, and cross-language comparisons. Breaking down the monolingual “target language only” bias.
