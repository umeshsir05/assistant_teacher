📘 Chapter 1

Learning and Acquisition

Language Pedagogy is the discipline concerned with the theories and techniques of teaching language. It has been described as a type of teaching wherein the teacher draws from his prior knowledge and actual experience in the language. It can be splitted into two paces:

  • Language Acquisition (भाषा अधिग्रहण)
  • Language Learning (भाषा अधिगम)

1. Acquisition (अधिग्रहण)

  • We acquire our mother tongue.
  • When a language is learnt without a systematic and conscious process, it is called an Acquisition. Generally the language is learnt without knowing grammar, rules and regulations in childhood period by Acquisition. Acquisition contains natural and subconscious process.
  • The environment plays an important role in the acquisition of language. The child is a passive recipient of external influence from parents, siblings and others.
  • Language acquisition is an informal process.
  • To learn a new language the children should increase their word power.
  • The language that a child acquires naturally from his home and society environmental is called that first language.
  • The child learns that first language through speech.
  • Language is a habit – this means language is acquired through rules use.
  • If them students come from a diverse background should discourage to use their mother-tongue.
  • If a person has just started learning a language and has acquired good command over grammar, it implies that he knows the rules of the language.

2. Learning (अधिगम)

  • Learning a language, is a process of learning Second Language in which learners learn the rules and regulations of grammar and vocabulary.
  • Persons’ learning experience is impacted through the capability and experience of teachers’.
  • For language learning children should be encouraged to read a lot of children literature in the target language because language is caught through plenty of exposure to reading.
  • The language family to which english belongs is knows as the Indo-European language family.
  • English language learning becomes easy input. rich environment is provided to the learners.

🧠 Types of Learning

Motor Learning (मोटर लर्निंग)

It is a change in the capability of a person to perform a skill that must be inferred from a relatively permanent improvement in performance as a result of practice or experience. It keeps Body movement: Walking, Driving, Climbing, etc.

Verbal Learning (मौखिक शिक्षा)

It is typically associated with the memorization and retention of lists of words. It keeps Communication methods: Speaking, sings, picture symbol, etc.

Introduction during an oral presentation is the stage where the speaker tells the audience about the aims of his presentation.

In oral communication what matters most is, how you say it as it is important for the listener to understand the main idea of communication.

Conceptual learning (वैचारिक शिक्षा)

It focuses more on understanding the concepts and learning how to organize and distribute data. It is a Mental process: Thinking, Reasoning, Intelligence, etc.

🏛️ Foundational Theorists

Jean Piaget

Jean Piaget – Father of child psychology (बाल मनोविज्ञान के जनक) – Swiss biologist, philosopher, psychologist.

According to Piaget: children start learning with adaptation (रूपान्तरण), assimilation (आत्मसात), accommodation (मेल). Children collect small unit of knowledge (ज्ञान की छोटी इकाई) and makes a bigger concept.

💡 Remark: Children create their own world of knowledge by their units of knowledge.

Noam Chomsky

Noam Chomsky – American linguist (भाषाविद), philosopher (दार्शनिक), cognitive scientist (संज्ञानात्मक वैज्ञानिक), historian (इतिहासकार), social critic (सामाजिक आलोचक).

According to Chomsky: children are born with inherited ability (जन्मजात योग्यता) to learn human language. Every person possesses LAD (Language Acquisition Device) (भाषा अधिग्रहण उपकरण) in their brain.

The role of language acquisition is to generate grammar rules.

💡 Remark: Children can not learn a language by imitation (नकल करना) only. Children learn vocabulary and LAD applies the rules of language to form a sentence, no need to learn grammar rules and structure. Children have inherited ability to understand the rule of language. Learning a new language after puberty leads to difficulty in the acquisition of a foreign language.

Lev Vygotsky

Work: foundation of cognitive theory (संज्ञानात्मक सिद्धांत की नींव), social development theory (सामाजिक विकास सिद्धांत).

According: trainer has better understanding or higher ability level than the learners, it is called MKO (More Knowledgeable Other). MKO stands for a person who may not be perfect in every field. Language is developed with social interaction (सामाजिक संपर्क). Language plays a critical role (महत्वपूर्ण भूमिका) in cognitive development (संज्ञानात्मक विकास).

ZPD (Zone of Proximal Development): is an important concept which denotes the difference between what a child achieves independently with guidance and encouragement by a skilled partner.

Scaffolding: It is one such educational practice by a powerful one that helps students to retain and apply new knowledge. MKO and ZPD are initially beyond the learner’s capacity.

🤝 Remark: He emphasized in Peer and Collaborative learning (साथ–साथ सीखना व सहयोग).

B.F. Skinner

Propounder: Conditioning theory – Operant conditioning theory. Operant conditioning theory is a method of learning that uses rewards and punishment to modify behaviour.

According: He believes that the best way to understand behaviour is to look at the causes of action and its consequences (परिणाम). Children learn language based on the behaviorist reinforcement principle (व्यवहारिक पुनर्बलन सिद्धांत) by associating.

📖 Grammar Translation Method

  • Grammar translation method of teaching english language is also called classical method.
  • The first language is maintained as the reference system in the acquisition of the second language in the grammar translation method.
  • Grammar teaching is more meaningful when grammar is taught in context.
  • Grammar can be taught inductively by giving examples to derive rules.
  • As a test of ability in speaking, listening, reading and writing the only favourable thing about translation is translation questions are easy to set.
  • The direct method is that classroom teaching should be done only in the target language.
Pedagogical Implication: Language acquisition is subconscious and natural, while learning is conscious and formal. Theorists like Piaget, Chomsky, Vygotsky, and Skinner provide complementary insights. Effective pedagogy blends rich input, social interaction, and explicit instruction.
