📘 Chapter 1

Learning and Acquisition

Language Pedagogy is the discipline concerned with the theories and techniques of teaching language. It has been described as a type of teaching wherein the teacher draws from his prior knowledge and actual experience in teaching language. It can be split into two parts:

  • Language Acquisition (भाषा अधिग्रहण)
  • Language Learning (भाषा अधिगम)

1. Acquisition (अधिग्रहण)

We acquire our mother tongue. When a language is learnt without a systematic and conscious process, it is called Acquisition. Generally the language is learnt without knowing grammar, rules and regulations in childhood period by Acquisition. Acquisition contains natural and subconscious process.

The environment plays an important role in the acquisition of language. The child is a passive recipient of external influence from parents, siblings and others. Language acquisition is an informal process.

  • To learn a new language the children should increase their word power.
  • The language that a child acquires naturally from his home and society environment is called first language.
  • The child learns that first language through speech.
  • Language is a habit – this means language is acquired through rules use.
  • If students come from a diverse background should discourage to use their mother tongue.
  • If a person has just started learning a language and has acquired good command over grammar, it implies that he knows the rules of the language.

2. Learning (अधिगम)

Learning a language is a process of learning Second Language in which learners learn the rules and regulations of grammar and vocabulary. Persons’ learning experience is impacted through the capability and experience of teachers.

  • For language learning children should be encouraged to read a lot of children literature in the target language because language is caught through plenty of exposure to reading.
  • The language family to which English belongs is known as the Indo-European language family.
  • English language learning becomes easy when a rich input environment is provided to the learners.

🧠 Types of Learning

Motor Learning (मोटर लर्निंग)
Change in capability to perform a skill (walking, driving, climbing) via practice or experience. Permanent improvement in performance.
Verbal Learning (मौखिक शिक्षा)
Memorization & retention of words; communication methods: speaking, songs, picture symbol, etc. Introduction during oral presentation tells audience aims. In oral communication, how you say it matters most.
Conceptual Learning (वैचारिक शिक्षा)
Understanding concepts, organizing data; mental processes: Thinking, Reasoning, Intelligence.

🏛️ Foundational Theorists

Jean Piaget

Father of child psychology (बाल मनोविज्ञान के जनक) – Swiss biologist, philosopher, psychologist.

According to Piaget: Children start learning with: Adaptation (रूपांतरण), Assimilation (आत्मसात), Accommodation (मेल). Children collect small unit of knowledge (ज्ञान की छोटी इकाई) and make a bigger concept.

💡 Remark: Children create their own world of knowledge by their units of knowledge.

Noam Chomsky

American linguist, philosopher, cognitive scientist, historian, social critic.

According to Chomsky: Children are born with inherited ability (जन्मजात योग्यता) to learn human language. Every person possesses LAD (Language Acquisition Device) in their brain. The role of language acquisition is to generate grammar rules.

  • Children cannot learn a language by imitation only.
  • Children learn vocabulary and LAD applies the rules of language to form sentences.
  • Children have inherited ability to understand the rules of language.
  • Learning a new language after puberty leads to difficulty in acquisition of a foreign language.

Lev Vygotsky

Foundation of cognitive theory – Social development theory.

According to: Trainer has better understanding or higher ability level than learners, it is called MKO (More Knowledgeable Other). MKO stands for a person who may not be perfect in every field. Language is developed with social interaction. Language plays a critical role in cognitive development.

ZPD (Zone of Proximal Development): It denotes the difference between what a child achieves independently and with guidance and encouragement by a skilled partner.

Scaffolding: It is an educational practice that helps students to retain and apply new knowledge. MKO and ZPD are initially beyond the learner’s capacity.

🤝 Remark: Emphasized peer and collaborative learning.

B.F. Skinner

Propounder – Conditioning theory, Operant conditioning theory. Operant conditioning theory is a method of learning that uses rewards and punishment to modify behaviour.

According to: The best way to understand behaviour is to look at causes of action and its consequences (परिणाम). Children learn language based on behaviourist reinforcement principle by associating.

📖 Grammar Translation Method

  • It is also called classical method.
  • The first language is maintained as the reference system in acquisition of second language.
  • Grammar teaching is more meaningful when taught in context.
  • Grammar can be taught inductively by giving examples to derive rules.
  • In speaking, listening, reading and writing, the only favourable thing is translation questions are easy to set.
  • Direct method: teaching should be done only in the target language.
Pedagogical Synthesis: Acquisition is subconscious, natural, and informal; Learning is conscious, systematic, and formal. Theorists like Piaget, Chomsky, Vygotsky, and Skinner provide complementary views. Effective pedagogy integrates rich input, social interaction, and explicit instruction.
