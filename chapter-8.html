Teaching-Learning Materials (TLM)

Audio Aids

Aids which appeal only to the ears are called audio aids.

Examples: Radio, Tape-recorder, Gramophone.

Visual Aids

Visual aids appeal only to the eyes.

Examples: Black board, Textbooks, Charts, Maps, Models, Picture, Films strips and slides, Flash-card, Flannel board etc.

Textbooks

The process of teaching and learning a language the textbook is one of the tools. Textbook should be supplemented with lots of outside reading.

  • Multimedia materials include all text, animation, image and audio.
  • Collage of paper cutting is not a multimedia material.
  • Group work gives a good cover for chat and gossip is not an advantage of teaching through a textbook.
  • Radio and television are one way communication.
  • Tents are the most suitable resource a teacher has for teaching vocabulary. (Note: likely “texts” or “contexts” but kept as original)
  • Multimedia materials are used to aid teaching and learning.

Audio-Visual Aids

The aids which appeal to both the eyes and the ears are called audio-visual aids.

Examples: Television, Cinema and Films.

Listening skill is involved when we watch a TV show.

Diagnostic Teaching (वैज्ञानिक शिक्षण)

Diagnosis means "to know the cause" in which the failure/deficiencies/difficulties of children are detected.

Pedagogical Insight: Teaching-learning materials (TLM) include audio aids (ear only), visual aids (eye only), and audio-visual aids (both). Textbooks are foundational but must be supplemented. Multimedia materials enhance engagement. Diagnostic teaching helps identify and address learning difficulties.
