📕 Chapter 4

Importance of Learning Skills

In English pedagogy, grammar is commonly defined as the set of rules and principles governing the structure of sentences, phrases, clauses, and words in a language. It encompasses the study of syntax (the arrangement of words and phrases to create well-formed sentences), morphology (the structure and form of words), and semantics (the meaning conveyed by words and their combinations).

“Grammar presents the facts of a language arranged under certain categories and deals only with what can be brought under general laws and stated in the form of general rules.” — Thomson and Loyalt
“If language is a vehicle of our thoughts and feelings, then grammar is the machine by which that vehicle is moving.” — IA Gordon

📖 Definitions of Grammar in English Pedagogy

1. Traditional Definition: Grammar is often defined traditionally as the system of language rules that enables speakers and writers to produce and interpret meaningful sentences. It includes rules for word order, verb conjugation, tense, agreement, punctuation and more.
2. Descriptive Definition: In a more descriptive sense, grammar refers to the way language is actually used by speakers and writers within a community or culture. This includes both formal grammar (rules taught in textbooks and classrooms) and informal grammar (how language is used in everyday communication).
3. Prescriptive Definition: Alternatively, grammar can be defined prescriptively, focusing on the rules and conventions deemed “correct” or “proper” within a particular dialect or standard of English. This approach often emphasizes adherence to established norms and conventions.
4. Functional Definition: In modern pedagogy, there's also a focus on the functional aspect of grammar, emphasizing how grammatical structures contribute to communication and meaning-making. This approach considers the communicative purposes served by different grammatical constructions.
5. Generative Definition: Some linguistic theories, such as generative grammar, define grammar as a system of rules that generate all and only the grammatical sentences in a language. This approach seeks to explain how speakers generate an infinite variety of grammatically correct sentences.

📚 Methods of Teaching English Grammar

Methods covered: Deductive Method, Inductive Method, Inductive-Deductive Method, Incidental Method

Deductive Method

This traditional approach involves presenting students with a set of rules about grammar, followed by practice exercises to apply those rules. The teacher provides explicit instruction using textbooks or grammar reference materials, and students apply the rules through drills, worksheets and sentence construction activities. This method is also called the traditional method.

In this method, grammar is taken as an independent subject and taught with the help of a grammar book. The teacher uses a grammar textbook. He first tells his students rules or definitions and then explains those with the help of examples then he gives exercises and asks his pupils to apply the rules.

The method is not very effective as it is against the principles of teaching and students find it boring and dull.

A. Advantages of the Deductive Method

  • An average teacher can use this method in countries like India.
  • It is based on the theory “From generalization to example”.
  • This method helps students to compare the ideas in the grammar of their mother tongue and their second language.
  • The learner can try the grammatical questions very easily.
  • The learner can respond effectively and can explain rules, structures etc.

B. Disadvantages of the Deductive Method

  • This method makes learners learn about language.
  • This method cannot develop the communicative ability among learners.
  • The learners become inactive during classroom teaching.
  • This method is not child-centred but teacher-centred.
  • In classroom teaching, there is hardly any use of audio-visual aids.

Inductive Method

In contrast to the deductive method, the inductive approach encourages students to discover grammar rules through examples and guided practice. The teacher presents authentic language examples or texts where students analyze patterns and draw conclusions about grammar rules. This method promotes critical thinking and active engagement in the learning process.

The inductive method is also known as the informal method. In the inductive method, the teacher first presents or takes the example from the students and then comes on the theory or concept.

This method implies teaching grammar not by rules but by usage. Through the continuous practice of using words while speaking, reading and writing, grammar can be taught and therefore learnt by students.

A. Advantages of the Inductive Method

  • The inductive method is based on the theory “From example to generalization”.
  • This method is very useful in classroom teaching (L2/FL).
  • This method helps students to understand the difference between particular notions in the grammar of L1 and L2.
  • This method is child-centred.
  • The learners learn the particular grammar point through use. First, they have to deduce the meaning and later they generalize the form or structure.

B. Disadvantages of the Inductive Method

  • This method is not useful in crowded classes like India.
  • The institute must be ready to focus on the language aspect, not the mark criteria.
  • The institute must be ready to focus on the language.
  • Only an innovative teacher can use this method.

Inductive-Deductive Method

This method of teaching grammar proves very successful and advantageous as it becomes practical, real and scientific. It follows all the maxims of teaching and pupils are not forced to cram the rules. This method also stimulates the power of thinking and reasoning.

The shortcomings of this method are that it can be applied only to young learners. Moreover, this method is not complete in itself because sometimes students are unable to correlate examples with the topic.

Through this method, students formulate rules with the help of examples.

Steps:

  1. Students are given some examples of similar types.
  2. Students try to find out similarities by analyzing or observing these examples.
  3. Students are asked to draw some conclusions.
  4. Then the teacher will give the rules and give new examples and ask pupils to verify the rules.

Incidental Method

This method is also known as the correlation or reference method of teaching. This method helps students to correlate grammar with other related logical structures. Students gain practical knowledge of grammatical rules.

Disadvantages:

  • It interferes with normal teaching.
  • Productive learning is less.
  • Sometimes creating or forming an incident related to a topic may be difficult for the teacher.

🗣️ Importance of Grammar in the Development of Communication Skills

  • Grammar helps in learning correct pronunciation.
  • With good grammar spoken or written words gain their meaning and value.
  • Knowledge of grammar improves skill of expression.
  • Grammar is also helpful in increasing accuracy. Grammar frames the mind to habits of order and clearness and also to logic and rhetoric. So, grammar rules can help learners develop a habit of thinking logically and clearly.

✍️ Importance of Grammar in the Development of Writing Skill

  • The learner learns to write with correct punctuation and correct language and spelling.
  • With the knowledge of grammar, parts of speech etc., the child develops an effective writing style.
  • Expression of feelings, emotions, and frustrations in an impressive manner is possible only by knowledge of grammatical rules, syntax, vocabulary etc.

🎧 Kinds of Listening

1. Discriminative Listening

Discriminative listening is when the listener interprets and assigns meaning to sound rather than to words. In discriminative listening, the listener interprets the differences and nuances of sounds and body language. The listener is sensitive to attributes including rate, volume, pitch and emphasis in speaking.

2. Comprehensive Listening

Comprehensive listening is about interpreting the words and ideas of the speaker. In essence, this type of listening requires that the listener understands the language and vocabulary. When it comes to comprehensive listening, having an understanding of basic language skills and vocabulary will not only help you follow along but communicate with the speaker.

💬 Kinds of Speaking Situations

Interactive (पारस्परिक)

Interactive communication is an exchange of ideas where both participants, whether human, machine or art form, are active and can have an effect on one another. It is a dynamic, two-way flow of information.

A naturalistic language sample can be obtained by interacting with the student while playing a game or having a conversation.

Partially Interactive (आंशिक संवाद)

When giving a speech to a live audience, where the convention is that audience does not interrupt the speech. The speaker nevertheless can see the audience and judge from their facial expressions and body language whether or not he or she is being understood.

Non-Interactive (गैर-संवादात्मक)

Few speaking situations can be totally non-interactive such as when recording a speech for a radio broadcast.

Pedagogical Insight: Grammar is the structural backbone of language. Teaching methods range from deductive (rules-first) to inductive (examples-first), and the inductive-deductive blend balances discovery with guidance. Incidental teaching integrates grammar naturally. Grammar supports pronunciation, expression, accuracy, and effective writing. Discriminative and comprehensive listening, along with interactive, partially interactive, and non-interactive speaking situations, complete the communicative picture.
