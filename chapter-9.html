Remedial Teaching (उपचारात्मक शिक्षण)

Remedial means – Rectify or improve something.

  • Remedial teaching is the teaching or instructional work carried out to provide remedial measures to help the pupils to get rid of their common or specific weaknesses. [CG Vyapam (TET-3) 2019]
  • Remedial teaching is designed to support students who lag behind in academic performance. [CG Vyapam (TET-2) 2022]
  • Remedial teaching is important for students to provide additional help to the students who are slow at learning. [CG Vyapam (TET-3) 2012]
  • Remedial Teaching is a multifaceted approach, tailoring remedial intervention plans to a child's specific needs. It makes use of one-on-one instruction, small group instruction, written work, verbal work and computer-based work.
  • Remedial Teaching is a part of formative assessment. [CG Vyapam (TET-3) 2017]
  • The 'gap' developed between teaching and learning can be best filled by using Remedial Teaching. [CG Vyapam (TET-2) 2017]
  • Remedial teaching is helpful for students who lag behind in certain areas. [CGVyapam (Asst. TEC) 2019]
  • Remedial teaching is meant to address gaps in learning. [CG Vyapam (TEC) 2019]
  • When you correct each and every mistake of your students they are demotivated. [CG Vyapam (TEC) 2019]

Objectives of Remedial Teaching

  • To develop good qualities in the students.
  • To provide learning support to pupils who are not as good as their peers in performance.
  • To remove the mental tension of the students.
  • To help with school curriculum and teaching strategies.
  • To increase the ability.
  • To improve their overall academic performance.
  • To help students in regular schools cope with the prescribed school curriculum.
  • A teacher provides learning activities and practical experiences to pupils according to their abilities and requirements.
  • To improve initial errors.
  • To help students develop study skills.
  • To remove students knowledge-related errors.
  • To teach the students necessary habits.
  • To give undesirable interest and point of views of the students a desirable form. [CG Vyapam (TET-3) 2022]

Remedial teaching required in the domain of ELT

  • Is in an inclusive approach.
  • Every learner has a varied pace of learning.
  • Little bit of scaffolding helps the slow learners to come into the mainstream.

Remedial Therapy

Remedial Therapy focuses on skills rather than on content. Perceptual organisation, laterality, sequencing, abstract reasoning, auditory – these skills include. [CGVyapam (Asst. TEC) 2019]

In diverse classroom it is best to use a wide variety of teaching strategies.

Principles of Remedial Teaching

  • Methods and materials – Early success for the pupil in his backward subject or subjects by use of suitable methods and materials.
  • Preparation – There needs to be enough emphasis on identifying the diverse learning.
  • Activities – Learning activities to develop the latent learning abilities.
  • Situations – It is good to create conducive learning situations.
  • Frequent planned remedial lessons.
  • Co-operation with the parents.

Process of Remedial Teaching

  • Remedial sessions.
  • Homework policies.
  • Curriculum Adaptation.
  • Formulation of teaching plans.
  • Categorising the problem areas.
  • Teaching activities, aids and supporting materials.

Strategies for Remedial Teaching

  • Reward Scheme
  • Peer Support Program
  • Individualised Educational Program
  • Handling Pupils' Behaviour Problems
Pedagogical Insight: Remedial teaching addresses learning gaps through tailored interventions. It is part of formative assessment, focuses on skills, and uses diverse methods, materials, and strategies. Scaffolding, parental cooperation, and positive reinforcement help slow learners progress.
