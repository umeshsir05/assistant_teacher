📗 Chapter 2

Principles of Language Teaching

Listening, Speaking, Reading and Writing are involved in learning a language. They are based on following well defined principles.

Motivation (प्रेरणा)

Learners can be Motivated to learn a language. It plays an important role in learning a language.

When someone says, "you did a good job", it means that he is appreciating you.

Interest (रुचि)

The teacher should generate a great deal of energy and interest among learners so they may pay attention to learning a language.

Imitation (नकल करना)

The children learn their mother tongue by imitation. The English teacher must provide a good model of speech before the learners and Audio-Visual aids should be used as well.

Co-relation (सह-संबंध)

Unless teaching and learning of English becomes co-related with practical life, learners will not realize the need of language learning and will not take interest in it.

Accuracy (विशुद्धता)

The English teacher should make accuracy while speaking, teaching and writing English language so that learners should follow their teacher and consider him as a role model.

Purpose (उद्देश्य)

There should be a definite purpose of learning a language so that learning may be easy and suitable.

Acustom with Language (भाषा का आदत बनाना)

When English is being learnt, it should be acustomed with learning so that Listening, Speaking, Reading and Writing habits are to be formed consciously and unconsciously.

Oral Approach (मौखिक दृष्टिकोण)

The Oral Approach or Situational Language Teaching is based on a structural view of language. Speech structures and a focus on a set of basic vocabulary items are seen as the basis of language teaching.

📌 Additional Key Concepts

Natural Order of Learning (सीखने का प्राकृतिक क्रम)

Listening-Speaking-Reading-Writing is the natural order of learning a language. In this order, a child learns his or her mother tongue without any formal instruction. So this natural order of learning should be considered while teaching English.

Selection and Gradation (चयन और उन्नयन)

One should proceed from simple to difficult in language learning; so vocabulary and structures of language should be selected and graded as per its frequency, teachability and difficulty level.

Language is ordered into five system of rules: phonology, morphology, syntax, semantics and pragmatics.

Children use language as a tool -

  • To communicate and they store information.
  • To maintain their relation with the society.
  • To understand apply the words and sentences and generate new ideas.
Pedagogical Insight: Effective language teaching integrates motivation, interest, imitation, correlation with real life, accuracy, clear purpose, habit formation, and the oral approach. Following the natural order (LSRW) and careful selection/gradation of content ensures better learning outcomes.
