📔 Chapter 5

Challenges of Teaching a Language

🌍 Effect of Regional Languages (क्षेत्रीय भाषाओं का प्रभाव)

Regional Language

India is a multilingual country (बहुभाषी देश). Different regions have their own languages and regional languages have an impact on the children, which is a rigid challenge to teach.

Challenge to develop linguistic skills

Students may face challenges in the development of language skill due to linguistic diversity, as regional language influences may also have an impact on linguistic skills.

💡 In teaching English, a multilingual classroom is an asset. [CGVyapam (TET-3) 2016]

Problem of Multiple Language

If a teacher is asked to teach in other area away from his native where the language that the teacher does not know, it is a challenge to teach.

Lack of Interest

One of the problems of language teaching is that there is a lack of interest and aptitude towards the language in students, thus task of language teaching becomes more challenging.

Overcrowded classes

In these types of classes a teacher cannot reach to every student and cannot rectify his mistakes.

Teaching plans

A teacher plan is a document that outlines the structure and details of a single session. If teacher do not prepare teaching plans, the teacher will not be clear about the objectives and materials required for teaching. [CGVyapam (TET-2) 2022]

A teacher needs to develop clarity about the objectives of a teaching plan before teaching. [CGVyapam (TET-3) 2016]

⚠️ Learning Disabilities & Disorders

Dyslexia (वाक्विकार)

[CGVyapam (TET-3) 2021]

  • Child is not able to learn a language mainly because of dyslexia.
  • Dyslexia is the disability with reading.
  • Children face difficulty in recognition of word, letters and other symbols. [CGVyapam (TET-2) 2017]
  • An example of a possible phonological processing disorders is saying sin for shin. [CGVyapam (TET-2) 2019]
  • Through drilling the teacher can improve the language skills of students and make them confident while speaking. [CGVyapam (TET-2) 2019]
  • A student makes mistake while speaking in class then he should not be discouraged by the teacher. [CGVyapam (TET-2) 2019] [CGVyapam (TET-3) 2017]
  • Speech disorders: Voice disorders, speed sound disorders, fluency disorders. [CGVyapam (TET-2) 2019]
  • Morphological disorder is not a speech disorder.

Dysgraphia (लेखन विकार)

[CGVyapam (TET-3) 2019]

  • Dysgraphia is the disability with writing.
  • Children face difficulties with spelling.
  • They have poor handwriting and they will also face difficulties in organizing ideas.

Dyscalculia (गणितीय अक्षमता)

  • Dyscalculia is the disability with Mathematics.
  • They have poor sense of direction, computing problem and problem with numbers.
  • Children with Dyscalculia face difficulty in problem solving, recalling number sequences.

Dyspraxia (डिस्प्रेक्सिया)

It is a developmental disorder of the brain in childhood causing difficulty in activities requiring coordination and movement.

Aphasia (अफेसिया) (वाचाघात)

  • A language disorder that affects a person's ability to communicate.
  • It can occur suddenly after a stroke or head injury or develop slowly from a growing brain tumour or disease.

Dysmorphia (डिस्मॉर्फिया)

  • Dysmorphia is a mental illness involving obsessive focus on a perceived flaw in appearance.
  • The flaw may be minor or imagined. But the person may spend hours a day trying to fix it.
  • The person may try many cosmetic procedures or exercise to excess.

Educational characteristics of learning disabled children

[CGVyapam (TET-2) 2021]

  • Child is unable to write the letters correctly.
  • He is poor in memorizing words and framing sentences.
  • They show poor coordination of motor abilities.
  • He is poor in technical and professional subjects.
  • Unreasonable difficulty in remembering verbal instructions.
Pedagogical Insight: Language teaching in multilingual contexts faces challenges like regional language influence, lack of interest, overcrowded classes, and inadequate planning. Specific learning disabilities (dyslexia, dysgraphia, dyscalculia, dyspraxia, aphasia) require differentiated instruction. Teachers should use drilling for confidence, avoid discouraging mistakes, and adapt strategies to support diverse learners.
