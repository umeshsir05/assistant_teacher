📒 Chapter 6

Language Skills (भाषा कौशल)

  • The two components of language are grammar and vocabulary. [CG Vyapam (TET-3) 2012]
  • Language functions are useful for expressing oneself effectively in a particular situation. [CG Vyapam (TEC) 2019]
  • Teach reading and writing first, listening and speaking next. [CG Vyapam (TET-3) 2017]
  • The four basic language skills are – Listening, Speaking, Reading, Writing. [CG Vyapam (TET-3) 2017]
    📝 Handwritten note: "J Primary skills", "L S R W"
  • Listening and speaking are known as primary skills. [CG Vyapam (TET-2) 2021]
  • Language skills are best learnt by using them for communication. [CGVyapam (Asst. TEC) 2019]
  • Learning of Grammar should be integrated with listening, speaking, reading, writing. [CGVyapam (Asst. TEC) 2019]

The limitation of oral communication is that it is irreversible – what is said cannot be taken back. [CG Vyapam (TET-3) 2017]

🎧 Listening Skills (श्रवण कौशल)

  • If a person draws conclusions after listening to a story it is termed as inferential listening. [CG Vyapam (TET-2) 2019]
  • Listening is the ability to accurately receive and interpret messages in the communication process.
  • Listening is key to all effective communication. Without the ability to listen effectively, messages are easily misunderstood. [CG Vyapam (TET-3) 2019]
  • Listening skill is involved when we watch a TV show. [CG Vyapam (TET-2) 2017]
  • As a result, communication breaks down and the sender of the message can easily become frustrated or irritated. [CG Vyapam (TET-2) 2017]
  • The first objective of teaching English is to develop listening and speaking skills. [CG Vyapam (TET-3) 2017]
  • Gathering only facts without trying to understand the content while listening is a barrier to effective listening skill. [CG Vyapam (TET-3) 2017]

🗣️ Speaking Skills (वाक् कौशल)

  • Oral skills are speech enhancers that are used to produce clear sentences that are intelligible to an audience.
  • Oral skills are used to enhance the clarity of speech for effective communication.
  • Communication is the transmission of messages and the correct interpretation of information between people.
  • The effectiveness of speaking depends on the speaker's ability to use simple language. [CG Vyapam (TET-3) 2017]
  • Articulators – Any vocal organ that takes part in the production of a speech sound. The articulators are lip, tongue and teeth. [CG Vyapam (TET-3) 2017]
    The face is not an articulatory organ. [CG Vyapam (TET-3) 2017]
  • To test the speaking ability of students, a teacher should ask them to narrate incidents or events. [CG Vyapam (TET-2) 2019]
  • Narrating a story with proper stress and intonation is a subskill of speaking skill. [CG Vyapam (TET-2) 2019]
  • A person is said to be a good speaker when he is able to convey ideas and feelings. [CG Vyapam (TET-3) 2019]
  • The knowledge of paralinguistic attributes such as voice modulation and articulation helps to improve speaking skill. [CG Vyapam (TEC) 2019]

📖 Reading Skills (पठन कौशल)

  • Reading a subject and acquiring its meaning is called reading skill. Instead of listening, reading skill leads to permanent knowledge.
  • Reading longer text usually for one's own pleasure is known as extensive reading (विस्तृत अध्ययन). [CG Vyapam (TET-3) 2019]
  • Reading and writing skills are involved when we summarize a paragraph. [CG Vyapam (TET-2) 2019]
  • Skimming is quickly running one's eyes over a text to get the gist of it (overview). [CG Vyapam (TET-2) 2019]
  • Scanning – A student reads fast looking for specific information in a manual. [CG Vyapam (TET-2) 2019]
  • Fluency in reading means the ability to read a text with ease and expression.
  • When we want to best writing skills we test the ability of students to present the matter in a well-organized manner. [CG Vyapam (TET-3) 2019]
  • There are two types of reading: (i) Recitation, (ii) Silent reading.

✍️ Writing Skill (लेखन कौशल)

  • Expressing your thoughts or things by writing is called writing skill.
  • In this skill the language is stabilized and spelling or correctness is given utmost importance.
  • Dictation does not test some problems of inflection (मोड). [CG Vyapam (TET-3) 2017]
  • Indenting and punctuation are the sub skills of writing skills. [CG Vyapam (TET-3) 2019]
  • Grammar in learning a language for communicating ideas verbally and in written form provides techniques for writing. [CG Vyapam (TET-3) 2017]

🎤 Listening Situations

Interactive (पारस्परिक)

Interactive listening situations include face-to-face conversations and telephone calls, in which we are alternately listening and speaking and in which we have a chance to ask for clarification, repetition or slower speech from our conversation partner.
(Handwritten: Two-way)

Non-interactive (गैर-पारस्परिक)

Some non-interactive listening situations are listening to the radio, TV, films, lectures or sermons. In such situations, we usually don't have the opportunity to ask for clarification, slower speech or repetition.
(Handwritten: One-way)

💬 Speaking Situations

Interactive (पारस्परिक)

It includes face-to-face conversations and telephone calls. When you speak to prove your point to others then your speech is argumentative. [CG Vyapam (TET-2) 2021]

Partially Interactive (आंशिक संवाद)

When giving a speech to a live audience, where the convention is that the audience does not interrupt the speech. The speaker nevertheless can see the audience and judge from the expressions on their faces and body language whether or not he or she is being understood.

Non-interactive (गैर-पारस्परिक)

The non-interactive speaking can be defined as recorded speech such as when recording a speech for radio broadcast. To conclude, speaking can be formal or informal. Informal speaking is typically used with family and friends or people you know well.

📚 Reading Modes

Silent Reading

Reading written material silently without sounding is called silent reading.

Aloud Reading

When the written material is read aloud.

Pedagogical Insight: Language skills are integrated – listening and speaking (primary/oral), reading and writing (secondary). Effective teaching balances receptive and productive skills. Skimming, scanning, extensive reading, inferential listening, and articulation are key subskills. Real communication requires both interactive and non-interactive practice.
