📓 Chapter 7

Evaluation (मूल्यांकन)

Evaluation is the structured interpretation and giving of meaning to predicted or actual impacts of proposals or results. Evaluation forms an integral part of the whole learning process and is done at regular basis. It is closely related to educational objectives. It is a continuous process. The aim of both assessment and evaluation is to measure children's ability, perception and learning.

Through the evaluation, the objectives of the three sides (cognitive, emotional, functional) are measured.

The assessment is a process that occurs during evaluation. Assessment is a brief process and evaluation is a comprehensive process.

While evaluating essay of high school students, who have expressed ideas in a well organized manner.

A language teacher should tolerate errors while the learner communicates in the target language.

Evaluating language proficiency includes evaluating the skills of listening, speaking, reading and writing.

⭐ Importance of Evaluation

To Check

Evaluation helps the teacher to know whether the teaching methodology adopted was successful or not.

To Generate an Idea

It helps the teacher to know how successful students were in learning new skills i.e. LSRW.

Decision Making

Evaluation also helps the students to determine which area of learning require more attention.

To Improve Achievement

One of the main purposes of evaluation at the school stage is to help the learners improve their achievement in scholastic areas and to develop life skills and attitudes with reference to a larger context and canvas of life.

Steps of Evaluation

Evaluation is done through a systematic approach:

  1. Determine educational objectives
  2. Organise learning experiences
  3. Measure behavioural changes
  4. Perform tests

School Based Assessment (SBA)

This form of assessment is performed at the school level by teachers and educators.

  • It is child-centred and multi-dimensional. Thus, it improves the social, physical, emotional and intellectual development of children.
  • It provides a good idea to teachers about what the children have learnt, how they learn, what difficulties they face, what their interests are, and so on.

📝 Types of Assessment

1. Formative Assessment (रचनात्मक)

Assessment done while teaching is called formative assessment, it is the assessment or way to know about children.

2. Summative Assessment (योगात्मक)

Assessment done at the end of year is called summative assessment.

Continuous & Comprehensive Evaluation (CCE)

Comprehensive in CCE implies that all the aspects of a child's personality have to be observed.

Two areas of CCE are scholastic and co-scholastic.

It is a development process of assessment which emphasises on two fold objectives. The objectives are continuity in evaluation and assessment of broad based learning and behavioural outcomes on the other. Continuous and comprehensive evaluation refers to a system of school based evaluation of students that covers all aspects of student development.

Continuous (लगातार)

It refers to the regularity in the assessment, constructive feedback and frequency of tasks. The development of a child is a continuous process, thus it should be evaluated continuously.

Comprehensive (व्यापक)

It refers to both scholastic and non-scholastic areas of a student's growth. According to theory, school's function is to develop both the cognitive & non-cognitive abilities of a child.

Pedagogical Insight: Evaluation is a continuous, comprehensive process that measures cognitive, emotional, and functional objectives. It includes formative (ongoing) and summative (end-term) assessments. School Based Assessment (SBA) and CCE focus on holistic development – scholastic and co-scholastic – ensuring regular feedback and improvement.
